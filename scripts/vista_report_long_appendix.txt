APPENDIX H — FORMAL VERIFICATION COMPANION (FOR VISTA READERS)

H.1 Why bounded model checking appears in this project
Bounded model checking (BMC) searches for counterexamples within a finite temporal horizon. Unlike proof by induction, BMC does not automatically generalize to infinite depth; instead, it answers a precise question: “Is there a behavior of length at most k that violates the property?” For VISTA, SymbiYosys with an engine such as smtbmc provides this service on the wrapped module. When a property passes BMC at depth d, one should still document d and any fairness or reset assumptions, because a different depth or a different encoding of X/Z semantics can change outcomes.

H.2 Relationship between syntax, elaboration, and proof
Yosys read_verilog -formal performs parsing and elaboration suitable for many SystemVerilog subsets used in academic flows. syntax_ok in the evaluation report means the aggregate wrapper—including inferred declarations and embedded assertions—parses under the configured Yosys invocation. Parsing success is necessary but not sufficient for meaningful verification: a design can parse yet contain vacuous properties, contradictory constraints, or assumptions that trivialize failures. VISTA therefore reports both syntax_ok and SymbiYosys status, plus Gold Jaccard against VERT references.

H.3 Vacuity and the first clock cycle
Assertions checked too early can be vacuously true or false depending on initialization. VISTA’s synchronous wrapper introduces a _started-style guard so that assertion checking is deferred until after the first active clock edge in the constructed harness, reducing spurious first-cycle interactions between uninitialized state and aggressive implications. This is an engineering mitigation rather than a universal semantic rule; industrial flows may require stronger reset assumptions or explicit initialization proofs.

H.4 Don’t-care bits and four-valued logic
SystemVerilog and Verilog use X to represent unknown or don’t-care during simulation; formal tools may interpret case comparisons with X differently depending on encoding. Sample 82 in the VERT-100 evaluation illustrates a critical lesson: Gold Jaccard can be 1.0 while SymbiYosys reports fail, because the reference assertions and generated assertions match lexically but the case selector includes a literal with X bits. Mitigations include rewriting selectors to concrete binary patterns, adding assumptions that ban X on control nets, or proving properties under strengthened interpretation policies agreed with the tool vendor.

H.5 Greedy assertion filtering as a repair loop
When the model emits many properties, some may stress the parser or the formal front-end in combination with the wrapper. greedy_filter_asserts incrementally retains assertions while monitoring Yosys parse success of the entire wrapper. This is analogous to a maximal feasible subset heuristic in constraint satisfaction. The logged yosys_greedy_dropped_asserts counts how many clauses were discarded across the benchmark run; it should be interpreted as a tool-compatibility statistic, not as a statement that dropped assertions were “wrong.”

H.6 Train/eval prompt alignment
Instruction-tuned models are sensitive to delimiter strings and field ordering. VISTA standardizes on assertion_prompt_template_v2.txt for both training prompts and evaluation formatting. Misalignment historically collapsed useful generations even when the base model was capable. Teams extending VISTA should version-control templates and forbid silent drift between train and eval.

H.7 Effective batch size and optimizer stability
The finetune_assertions.py defaults emulate a large effective batch via gradient accumulation to stabilize loss landscapes for long-context examples. While VERT fragments are not always long, the maximum sequence length is set to 4096 to accommodate occasional lengthy RTL. Training logs should record actual GPU model, peak VRAM, wall time, and final validation loss for thesis reproducibility.

H.8 Web deployment and intellectual property
Local deployment (FastAPI + Next.js on the user’s machine) keeps RTL off third-party SaaS. For NECOP or other industrial pilots, air-gapped evaluation is a requirement; VISTA’s scripts support that mode when Hugging Face caches are populated. Document any outbound network policy exceptions.

H.9 Metric hygiene: Gold Jaccard is not “accuracy”
Token Jaccard compares bags of tokens between generated assertions and the dataset’s Assertion field. It does not measure semantic entailment, nor does it measure industrial coverage. Use the term “lexical similarity” in prose when caution is needed. High mean Jaccard with rare SBY failures is still an excellent outcome, but thesis language should avoid overstating “100% correctness.”

H.10 Roadmap to counterexample-guided refinement
When SymbiYosys fails, logs may include counterexample waveforms or signal valuations. A future CEGAR loop would parse these traces, map failing nets to RTL regions, and either revise assumptions or regenerate targeted assertions. This closes the loop between formal engines and LLM editing, moving from one-shot generation to iterative verification engineering.

APPENDIX I — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (PROJECT DEFENSE STYLE)

Q1. What exactly does VISTA automate?
A1. It automates drafting SystemVerilog Assertions from RTL fragments with VERT-style metadata, then deterministically wraps and checks them with open-source tools.

Q2. Why not use ChatGPT without fine-tuning?
A2. General models lack VERT’s idiom distribution; fine-tuning improves adherence to property formatting and naming patterns while post-processing enforces tool compatibility.

Q3. Is SymbiYosys a “proof” of the chip?
A3. No. It is formal analysis under stated bounds, assumptions, and engine capabilities. Results are conditional on those statements.

Q4. Why LoRA rank 256 in code when some drafts mention smaller ranks?
A4. The repository training script defaults are authoritative; earlier prose drafts may be outdated.

Q5. Can VISTA replace verification engineers?
A5. No. It assists drafting and regression packaging; engineers must validate assumptions and interpret failures.

Q6. What is the single failed case?
A6. Index 82: SBY fail with Jaccard 1.0; don’t-care case selector implicated.

Q7. How long does inference take?
A7. Depends on GPU and token count; the project targets interactive use on a high-end desktop GPU.

Q8. Does the web UI run SymbiYosys on every click?
A8. Deployment configurations vary; the evaluation pipeline always can. Document your deployment choice.

Q9. How do you prevent hallucinated signals?
A9. infer_signals merges RTL and assertion identifiers, filters keywords, and the wrapper declares inferred nets.

Q10. What if Yosys upgrades break parsing?
A10. Pin toolchain versions; re-run the 100-case regression and compare JSON reports.

Q11. Why greedy filter rather than fix the model only?
A11. Tool front-ends are brittle; a filter guarantees a parseable artifact for grading while preserving as many clauses as possible.

Q12. Is VERT public and license-compatible?
A12. Cite the exact VERT source and license in your final references; this report does not substitute for legal review.

Q13. Can we train on proprietary RTL?
A13. Only with corporate approval and scrubbing policies; otherwise use synthetic or open IP.

Q14. What about multi-clock designs?
A14. The current wrapper targets a single parsed clock specification per sample; multi-clock is future work.

Q15. Does VISTA support SVA sequences and properties in full IEEE form?
A15. The Yosys-centric path lowers some temporal forms; full temporal support may require vendor tools.

Q16. How do you compare to AssertionBench numbers?
A16. Different datasets and metrics; compare only after aligning task definitions and toolchains.

Q17. What GPUs were used?
A17. Record your actual hardware in the Word thesis; this Markdown generator does not read training logs.

Q18. Why Unsloth?
A18. Training throughput and memory optimizations for LLaMA-family fine-tuning.

Q19. Can assertions be ranked by importance?
A19. Not in the baseline; ranking is future work.

Q20. Is there a Docker image?
A20. If your team added Docker, document the compose file path; otherwise describe manual setup.
